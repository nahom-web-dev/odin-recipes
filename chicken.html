Chicken Arroz Caldo

Arroz caldo is a lugaw (glutinous rice dish) made with chicken and rice. It’s flavored with garlic, onions, ginger, fish sauce, and other seasonings. Though “arroz caldo” is a Spanish name, the dish’s origins are Filipino — it is an adaptation of a congee that was created by Chinese-Filipino immigrants.

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 (2 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 ¼ pounds chicken wings, split and tips discarded
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (Optional)
  • 5 ¼ cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup glutinous sweet rice
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1 lemon, sliced (Optional)
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce for sprinkling, if desired (Optional)

How to Make Arroz Caldo

  1. Cook the onion, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken and stir in the fish sauce.
  3. Pour in the broth and sweet rice, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Season and continue cooking until the chicken is fully cooked.

Chicken Makhani (Indian Butter Chicken)

This butter chicken recipe, or chicken makhani, is one of my favorite Indian dishes. It features a full-flavored sauce that complements the chicken well. It can be made as mild or spicy as you wish by adjusting the cayenne. Serve with basmati rice and naan bread

  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil, divided
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • ¼ white onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala, divided
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 cup half-and-half
  • ¼ cup plain yogurt
  • 1 pinch salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

How to Make Chicken Makhani (Indian Butter Chicken)

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt and spices.
  2. Sear the chicken in a hot pan until lightly browned.
  3. Sauté onions and spices in butter, then add tomato puree to create the sauce base.
  4. Blend the sauce until smooth (if desired).
  5. Add the cooked chicken, cream, and a knob of butter to the sauce.
  6. Simmer gently until the sauce thickens and the chicken is fully cooked.
  7. Garnish with cilantro and serve hot with naan or rice.
